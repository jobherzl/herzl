11 Real Estate Apps Useful For Investors

11 Real Estate Apps Useful For Investors

Mar 26, 2018 | Real Estate Investing |

Real estate apps make every business thrive while being very easy to use. When it comes to owning a business, working smart is as important as working hard. One way to work smart is to make certain tasks less time-consuming. That way, you’ll work more efficiently by saving both time and money. Fortunately, these real estate apps will help you and your business do exactly that. Equip your business and agents with these today!

Real Estate Apps to Download | Investing in the Digital Age

1. Zillow

Zillow is one of the most popular real estate apps, with over 70 million users every month. It is even said that if people are looking to rent, buy, or sell properties, they type in ‘Zillow’ on a Google search. It provides value estimates, value changes, and basic information across 110 million properties. No wonder it is one of the most loved apps in the real estate world.

2. Placester

This real estate app builds a website for all your available listings. This is essential for those who have more than a handful of properties they’re looking to lease. Placester also uses a WordPress platform so it is easy to use and configure as needed.

3. DocuSign


Docusign takes out the drudgery that comes with the heavy paperwork of contracts. This real estate app allows for an easier way for someone to sign off on documents. Also, did you know it’s used by over 250,000 companies all over the world?

4. CityMaps

This app is great for those who are very hands-on with their tenants, as City Maps provides them with recommendations and maps. Tenants will find this app very helpful in getting familiar with their new neighborhood.

5. Matterport

Matterport is perfect for tenants who want to view a listing without having to be physically present. This real estate app provides a 3D walk tour, making your listing more accessible and attractive than ever.

6. Zurple

Zurple helps investors and agents get to know a potential market more to give them an offer they simply can’t refuse. It tracks an individual’s browsing habits, noting their budget and preferences, and feeds them listings they are more likely to be interested in. As a result, this leads to more tenants and more profit for the real estate investor.

7. Smarter Agent Mobile

Mobile property manager apps are all the rage now, and Smarter Agent Mobile has taken notice. If an investor wants an app for his listings, all they have to do is pay Smarter Agent Mobile an affordable monthly fee. Soon, they will receive a pre-designed app customized with their branding, properties, and agents.

8. Prempoint

Tired of opening and closing a listing’s locks with a manual key? Use Prempoint instead. It’s a state of the art lock that not only grants agents and potential tenants access to the listing, but it allows for an easier download of shared documents as well.

9. MagicPlan

MagicPlan is recommended for real estate agents, as it helps them tailor fit a home according to the preference of the potential tenants. With MagicPlan, agents can easily map out a room and show where the different kinds of furniture can be placed around the house.

10. Realeflow

Whether you are a small time real estate investor or someone who has turned it into a business, this app will be an immense help. Realeflow manages leads, contacts, deals, properties, and even marketing campaigns and websites. The best part is, it also has a deal analyzer and a repair estimator.

11. CalcsFree

Calcsfree is another app an investor can equip their agents with because it makes calculations a breeze. After all, not everyone is a mathematician, but anybody can plug in a few numbers and read off how much the mortgage, taxes, and insurance payments will be.

 

Real estate apps and software were designed to help you run your business better. Although some of these products are not free, it is important to note that, sometimes, time saved is more valuable. A monthly fee or one-time charge is well worth preventing precious minutes from being wasted.

Are there any other real estate apps you use? Let us know in the comments below!
